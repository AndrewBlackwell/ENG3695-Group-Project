Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about your VORTEX-ONE drone.

General Questions

How long does the VORTEX-ONE battery last?
The VORTEX-ONE offers up to 46 minutes of flight time on a single charge under ideal conditions. Actual flight time may vary depending on wind conditions, flying style, and environmental temperature. We recommend landing when the battery level reaches 30% to ensure safe return.
Do I need to register my VORTEX-ONE drone?
Yes, in most countries including the United States, drones weighing over 250g (including the VORTEX-ONE) must be registered with aviation authorities. In the US, register with the FAA at FAA DroneZone. Requirements vary by country, so check your local regulations.
What is the range of the VORTEX-ONE?
The VORTEX-ONE features advanced O3+ transmission technology with a maximum transmission range of up to 15km in optimal conditions (FCC-compliant areas with no interference). Always maintain visual line of sight with your drone as required by regulations in most countries.
Is the VORTEX-ONE waterproof?
The VORTEX-ONE has an IP55 rating, which means it offers limited protection against dust and water spray. It is not waterproof and should not be flown in heavy rain, snow, or over bodies of water at low altitude. Always check weather conditions before flying.

Technical Questions

What is the maximum wind speed resistance?
The VORTEX-ONE can resist winds up to 12m/s (Level 6). However, for optimal stability and image quality, we recommend flying in wind conditions below 8m/s. The drone will issue warnings when wind speeds approach these limits.
How do I update the firmware on my VORTEX-ONE?
Firmware updates are managed through the VORTEX Fly app. Connect your drone and controller to the app, and you'll be notified when updates are available. Always ensure your drone and controller have sufficient battery (at least 50%) before starting an update, and don't interrupt the process.
What memory cards are compatible with VORTEX-ONE?
The VORTEX-ONE supports microSD cards with capacities up to 1TB. For optimal performance, use UHS-I Speed Grade 3 or higher cards. We recommend SanDisk Extreme Pro, Lexar Professional, or Kingston Canvas React Plus for 5.1K video recording.
Can I use third-party batteries with my VORTEX-ONE?
We strongly recommend using only official VORTEX batteries. Third-party batteries may be incompatible with the drone's power management system and safety features, potentially leading to flight failures or shortened drone lifespan. Using unauthorized batteries may also void your warranty.

Photography & Videography

What is the Hasselblad Natural Color Solution?
The Hasselblad Natural Color Solution (HNCS) is a proprietary color calibration system that ensures accurate, true-to-life colors with smooth tonal transitions. This technology maps colors precisely to how the human eye perceives them, resulting in stunning images with minimal post-processing required.
Can I shoot in RAW format with the VORTEX-ONE?
Yes, the VORTEX-ONE can capture still images in both JPEG and 12-bit RAW formats. RAW files (DNG format) retain more image data, giving you greater flexibility during post-processing for adjusting exposure, white balance, and color grading.
What is the best setting for low-light photography?
For optimal low-light performance, use Manual mode with these recommended settings: lower your shutter speed (1/30s or slower, using a tripod or hovering in place), increase ISO (400-1600 depending on conditions), and set aperture to f/2.8 on the wide lens. Shooting in RAW will also give you more latitude for noise reduction in post-processing.
How does ActiveTrack 5.0 work?
ActiveTrack 5.0 uses advanced machine learning algorithms to recognize and track subjects while navigating around obstacles. To use it, draw a box around your subject in the VORTEX Fly app. The system predicts subject movement and plans flight paths to maintain framing while avoiding obstacles. It works in all recording modes up to 5.1K/50fps.

Troubleshooting

My drone won't connect to the controller. What should I do?
Try these steps in order: 1) Ensure both devices are fully charged, 2) Restart both the drone and controller, 3) Re-link them by powering both off, then power on the drone first, followed by the controller, 4) Check for firmware compatibility in the VORTEX Fly app, 5) If problems persist, try resetting the connection in the app settings menu under "Control > Linking."
The compass needs calibration frequently. Is this normal?
Compass calibration requests are normal when flying in new locations or near structures with magnetic interference. However, frequent requests in the same location may indicate issues. Keep the drone away from metal objects during calibration, and if problems persist, check for magnetic items attached to the drone or consider a compass sensor check through customer support.
My videos have a jello effect or are shaky. How can I fix this?
Jello effect is usually caused by vibrations affecting the gimbal. Check that your propellers are undamaged and properly balanced. Ensure the gimbal is calibrated through the app settings. When filming, use slower flight speeds and gentler control inputs. If persisting, run the gimbal auto-calibration in the app under "Camera > Gimbal > Auto Calibration."
The obstacle avoidance system keeps triggering even when there are no obstacles. What's wrong?
This could be due to dirty sensors, flying over reflective surfaces (water, glass, etc.), or in low-light conditions. Clean the sensors with a soft brush or microfiber cloth, avoid flying over highly reflective surfaces, or temporarily disable obstacle avoidance in the app (only do this in open areas and fly with extreme caution).
